ABOUT ME

I am currently pursuing a Doctorate in Public Administration, focused on Health and Research Policy (ABD) at the University of Baltimore. Previously, I attended Johns Hopkins University and obtained a Master’s of Science in Biotechnology, with a concentration in Biodefense. I have over 10 years of experience regulating Federal and Department of Defense (DoD) human subjects research (HSR) policies and HSR operations.

Currently, I support the Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency’s (DARPA), as a scientist, in maintaining their human subjects research protection program by ensuring that all Federal and DoD policies and regulations are followed with the highest ethical standards. I regularly participate as a subject matter expert on Assistant Secretary of Defense Research and Engineering Directorate (ASD R&E) panels for Federal and Defense human subjects research policies at conferences and meetings.

Previously, I worked as a human subjects protection scientist at Walter Reed Army Institute of Research (WRAIR), where I obtained and demonstrated deep knowledge of HSR and DoD policies and their application. I have assisted multiple Institutional Review Boards (IRBs), Headquarters Level Review IRBs, and other domestic and international research ethical committees in obtaining approval and maintaining regulatory compliance for a variety of research activities.

My ethical and regulatory experience is further complimented by my certifications, work experience, and educational background. I am currently working on my dissertation titled “Ethical, Legal and Societal Implications of Neuroscience and Technology Research and its Impact on Public Policy,” with expected completion in early 2017.
